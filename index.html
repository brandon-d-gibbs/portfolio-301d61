About Me

I haven't always been a software developer. I remember tinkering with html and inline styling with my AOL homepage, my geocities site, a few other little blog sites, and of course, MySpace. But, life led me down a different path.
I spent four years as an active duty Marine which had me deploy to Afghanistan. That time in my life taught me a lot about dedication, sacrifice, and the drive and sheer will to accomplish tasks. It taught me the importance of teamwork and communication as well. While I was state-side, my job as a Marine afforded me the opportunity to take courses and trainings to really build skills that I find to be invaluable tools for communication and leadership.
After leaving active duty, I got into sales, which led to a position as Director of Marketing and Social Media. The close relationship I developed with our site development team resparked my interest in the web. I left that job after some time, when I had the chance to start my own business online in the financial space. I worked very closely with the guy that built our first site, but I didn't like where it ended up, so I dove in and built the second version myself around our 1 year mark. Thus began an itch that I have not been able to shake.
Since then, I have dedicated myself to a journey to become a full-stack software developer. I want to build things that make peoples lives better in some way. Whether that gives them back some of their time, their money, or simply just makes a day to day task easier.
